Frequently Asked Questions

In the United States, the standard tip at a sit-down restaurant is 15–20% of the pre-tax bill. For excellent service, 20–25% is customary. For buffets or counter service, 10% is typical. In many other countries, tipping customs vary — some include service charges automatically, while in places like Japan, tipping is not expected at all.
Technically, tips should be calculated on the pre-tax amount since the tip is for the service, not the government tax. However, many people find it simpler to tip on the total including tax, and the difference is usually small. Our calculator defaults to pre-tax but gives you the option to include tax with a simple toggle.

Quick Tipping Tips

Always check if a service charge is already added to your bill before tipping.

Tip based on the pre-tax subtotal for the most accurate amount.

For large parties (6+), restaurants often add 18% gratuity automatically.

When splitting, always calculate tip on the full bill, not per-person shares.

Cash tips go directly to your server — consider tipping in cash when possible.
